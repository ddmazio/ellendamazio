Zine

Typography
2023

Created entirely without digital tools, this zine was born from a collective desire to explore identity, perception, and the blurry line between appearance and reality. Built in collaboration with three other designers, the project challenged us to construct a compelling narrative through purely analog means — embracing imperfection, texture, and intuition.

We live in a world where comparison has become second nature. We seem happy, but we’re not. Likewise, we seem successful, but something feels off. Through visual metaphors and layered storytelling, the zine invites readers to slow down and reflect: what’s real, what’s performative, and what’s simply assumed? Every page is meant to provoke doubt, and in that doubt, space for personal interpretation.

This project is a reminder that design doesn’t need to shout to be powerful. Sometimes, rawness and ambiguity speak louder. The zine offers no clear answers, only questions, and in doing so, becomes a quiet protest against surface-level narratives and a celebration of complexity. This project was developed within the Communication Design I module of the Communication Design program at FBAUL.
