All in a Chain

Motion Design
2023

We reimagined the opening credits of Martin Scorsese's After Hours (1985), visually conveying the chaos, anxiety, and surrealism that define Paul Hackett’s night. Inspired by the film’s symbolic language, we turned key objects, like a $20 bill and a cardboard sculpture, into animated protagonists, translating narrative tension into graphic motion.

Guided by a red LED line that symbolizes Paul’s route through the city, the sequence maps his impulsive choices and the intersecting paths of the women who alter his course. The visual system draws from New York’s nightscape — subway lines, neon lights, and the emotional dissonance between escapism and exhaustion. The art direction blends 1930s-inspired type with the eclectic signage of 1980s Manhattan, capturing the film’s layered urban atmosphere.

Color, rhythm, and subtle sound effects were designed to evoke a sense of disorientation and unease. More than a stylistic exercise, the sequence offers a symbolic and emotional extension of the film, proving how motion design can deepen narrative through visual metaphor.
