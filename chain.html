All in a Chain

TIMELINE Spring 2024
TEAM Maria do Carmo Almeida, Maria Silva
SCOPE Motion
WATCH Watch on YouTube

We reimagined the opening credits of Martin Scorsese's After Hours (1985), translating the film's chaos, anxiety, and surrealism into graphic motion. Key objects — like a $20 bill and a cardboard sculpture — become animated protagonists, visually echoing Paul Hackett's increasingly erratic journey. Guided by a red LED line that symbolizes his path through the city, the sequence reflects impulsive choices and the intersecting routes of the women who shift his course.

Visually rooted in New York's nightscape, the design draws from subway systems, neon lights, and the tension between escapism and exhaustion. The art direction blends 1930s-inspired typography with the visual noise of 1980s Manhattan, capturing the film's layered atmosphere. Through rhythm, color, and subtle sound design, the credits function not just as introduction but as emotional prologue — showing how motion design can extend narrative through metaphor and mood.
