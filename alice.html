Alice in Wonderland

A reinterpretation of Alice's Adventures in Wonderland that explores the absurd, curiosity, and instability of the original narrative universe. It stems from the intention to give this world-renowned children's story a different, less childlike tone, using typography not merely as text but as an active visual element — through shifts in scale, cuts, and misalignments that evoke Alice's sense of displacement.

The contrasting colour palette and unconventional composition enhance the playful yet unsettling nature of the story, while the layout choices deliberately break away from traditional linear reading. The result is an editorial object that invites the reader to visually "lose themselves," reflecting the chaotic and imaginative spirit of Wonderland.
