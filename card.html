Overview

A business card is usually a direct invitation — a name, a contact, a sentence that sums up who you are. This one starts from the same premise and works against it: a hyphenated professional profile shares the screen with a grid of muted social media clips that scroll and multiply as you read, replaying the same attention-fragmentation the text names out loud.

The profile itself — interaction-designer-chronically-curious-about-how-interfaces-shape-human-behavior-researching-the-science-of-attention-and-still-getting-lost-by-scrolling-for-hours-with-music-in-her-ears — was written for the individual card component of “A Designer at the End of…”, a briefing that asked each student to define a self-positioning statement within the field of communication design. Rather than illustrating the sentence, the card performs it: the more you scroll, the more the videos crowd the frame, until “curious” and “still getting lost” stop being a description and become the actual experience of reading it.
