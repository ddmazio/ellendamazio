Let's Talk!

ting

The Path of Wayfinding Systems in the UK
UI/UX Design
2025

In a world defined by hyperconnectivity, loneliness has emerged as a silent epidemic—especially among Generation Z. Despite constant access to messaging apps, social media, and dating platforms, many young people report feeling increasingly disconnected. Our initial research revealed a paradox: digital spaces often heighten anxiety and diminish confidence, prioritizing performance and appearance over presence and authentic connection.

To address this, we designed Ting—a wearable that restores spontaneity and emotional presence in human interactions. Worn at the top of the spine, near the vagus nerve, Ting senses emotional patterns through biometric data like heart rate, posture, and temperature. When someone nearby shares compatible emotional signals, it emits a gentle vibration—a private, intuitive prompt to connect, without photos, profiles, or pressure.

Paired with a mobile app powered by emotional AI, Ting learns from your rhythms and offers weekly insights to support emotional awareness and real-world engagement. By turning the body into a subtle emotional interface, Ting proposes a new paradigm for connection—rooted in trust, resonance, and embodied experience rather than algorithmic performance.
